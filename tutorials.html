Everyday Edits

Fix a Typo or Change Text

Beginner 5–10 min

The most common job there is: a word is wrong on the live site, and you want it fixed. This works for any text anywhere — headlines, paragraphs, button labels, footer lines.

✅ Before you start
  • You know which page the text is on (look at the live site if unsure).
  • You can open your website folder in your file explorer.
  • If you're on a different computer or browser than your last editing session: have your live config.js handy — step 2 needs it.
  1. Open the admin. In your website folder, double-click admin.html. It opens in your browser — this is your private control panel; nothing here touches the live site yet.
  2. Make sure the admin is up to date. If this is the same browser you always edit in, the admin remembers your last session — skip ahead. If it's a new computer or browser, drag your site folder's config.js onto the dashed "Drag & drop your config.js here" zone at the top. The admin now matches the live site exactly.
  3. Find the text. Use the admin's sidebar to open the page the text lives on — Home Page, About Us, or one of your custom pages. Click a section's title bar to expand it, and look through its fields until you spot the sentence you're after. Fields hold exactly what the site shows, so the wording will look familiar.
  4. Make the edit. Click into the field and fix the text, just like editing any form. Watch the top bar: within a couple of seconds it will show an "Autosaved" message — that's the admin confirming it caught your change.
  5. Preview it. Click 💾 Save & Preview in the top bar. The site opens in a new tab — go to the page and read your corrected text in place. This preview is visible only to you.
  6. Publish it. Click ⬇ Export config.js — a fresh config.js lands in your Downloads folder. Move it into your website folder, replacing the old one (choose yes when asked about overwriting). If your site is hosted online, upload that same file to your host. Reload the live site: fixed.
Check your work

Open the live site (not the preview tab) and reload the page — on most computers Ctrl+F5 / Cmd+Shift+R forces a fresh copy. If the new wording shows, you're done. While the export is still in hand, copy it into your backup folder with today's date — publishing and backing up make one habit.

If the change isn't showing

Nine times out of ten the new config.js didn't actually replace the old one — check the file's "date modified" in your website folder. The Publishing Changes tutorial covers this step in richer detail.
